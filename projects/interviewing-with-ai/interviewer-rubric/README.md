# Interviewer Rubric

How to evaluate candidates in AI-assisted technical interviews.

## Structure

This rubric has two parts:

### Part 1: AI Experience
Candidate's background, knowledge, and track record with AI.
- **File:** `01-ai-experience.md`
- **When:** Early in the interview (intro/discussion)
- **Purpose:** Understand their claimed experience and foundation
- **Note:** Claims will be verified during solution execution (Part 2)

### Part 2: Solution Execution
How candidates apply AI experience and core principles while solving the problem.
- **Files:** `02-requirements-and-design.md`, `03-implementation.md`, `04-testing.md`
- **When:** Throughout the interview as they work through phases
- **Purpose:** Observe if claimed experience translates to effective problem-solving
- **Note:** Assess how well they demonstrate core principles in action

## Core Principles (Evaluation Lens)

When evaluating Part 2, look for these core principles:
1. **You actively direct the AI** — push back, reject, prioritize
2. **Articulate your thinking out loud** — speak what you're gaining/learning
3. **Work through the problem WITH the AI** — collaborate, not outsource
4. **Control the narrative** — ask constraining questions, guide scope
5. **Ask AI to explain and compare** — accelerate understanding with familiar concepts

## Assessment Scale

Each dimension uses: **Low / Medium / High**

- **Low:** Struggling, missing fundamentals, red flags
- **Medium:** Capable, demonstrates competence, room to grow
- **High:** Strong, demonstrates mastery, clear growth trajectory

## For Candidates

This rubric is public. Study it alongside the candidate guide to understand what interviewers are evaluating.
