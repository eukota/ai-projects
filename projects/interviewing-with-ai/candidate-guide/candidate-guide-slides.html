Core Principles

  • You actively direct the AI — push back, reject, prioritize
  • Articulate your thinking out loud — speak what you're gaining
  • Work WITH the AI — collaborate, not outsource
  • Control the narrative — ask constraining questions
  • Ask AI to explain — compare to familiar concepts

"Industry is coalescing around evaluation axes: problem decomposition, control over the AI (directing vs. being directed), verification/testing habits, and keeping the interviewer in the loop."

Core Principle: Active Direction

❌ Poor Prompt

❌ Outsourcing:
"Write me a URL shortener."

Why it fails: One-shot. You've surrendered every decision to the AI.

✅ Excellent Prompt

✅ Directing:
"We're designing a URL shortener. List the open requirements questions that most change the design — custom aliases, expiration, analytics, auth, scale. Group by architectural impact."

Why it works: You use AI to surface options, then you make the calls. You're steering toward completable.

Core Principle: Articulate Thinking

❌ Poor Approach

❌ Silent:
Read AI response. Say nothing. Move on.

Why it fails: Interviewer has no signal that you're thinking critically.

✅ Excellent Approach

✅ Speaking out loud:
"I'm seeing three approaches here. I like the base62 counter because it gives collision-free codes with trivial logic. The tradeoff is guessability — acceptable for this."

Why it works: Interviewer hears your reasoning in real-time. Shows mastery and confidence.

Core Principle: Work WITH the AI

❌ Poor Approach

❌ Black box:
"Now write all the code." Accept output. Done.

Why it fails: You get code you didn't shape and can't defend.

✅ Excellent Approach

✅ Collaborative:
"Let's build in slices. Start with just shorten(url) and resolve(code). Use in-memory dict. No web layer yet — I want to eyeball the core logic first."

Why it works: You decompose, delegate a slice, keep design yours. Actually review ~20 lines.

Core Principle: Control the Narrative

❌ Poor Approach

❌ Vague bolt-on:
"Add expiration too."

Why it fails: Expiry means nothing—AI guesses.

✅ Excellent Approach

✅ Scoped and specific:
"Add optional expiry: shorten(url, ttl_seconds=None). On resolve, treat expired as not-found. Lazy expiry is fine. Don't leak whether code ever existed."

Why it works: You specify behavior, interface, and security nuance. You guide the work toward completable.

Core Principle: Ask AI to Explain

❌ Poor Approach

❌ Silent acceptance:
AI generates code. You move forward without understanding.

Why it fails: Gaps in understanding. You can't defend choices.

✅ Excellent Approach

✅ Ask & compare:
"Explain the base62 counter increment. Compare it to how a relational DB auto-increment works."

Why it works: You build mental model by comparing to familiar. You own the solution.

Requirements & Design

The Approach

Timeline: 10-15 minutes total

  • Type focused prompt directly into AI chat
  • Clear statement of design goal
  • Name the AI process/tool you want
  • List requirements and constraints
  • Ask interviewer clarifying questions in real-time
  • Read critically and call out what you don't want
  • Work with AI toward refined plan through iteration
Requirements & Design

Interview Signaling

  • Opening move: "AI could spend 4 hours designing, but this is an interview, so I'm jumping in by giving requirements and getting a quick plan"
  • As you read: Speak out loud about what you're gaining from the AI response
Requirements & Design

Advanced Strategies

  • Bring your own design process — but compress for interview time (risk: 30 min on 5-min work)
  • Spawn background AI research — must circle back and incorporate results
  • Devote more time to plan — only if you know your system implements quickly (risk vs. credibility boost)
Implementation

The Approach

  • Tell AI to execute the agreed plan
  • Constrain for speed: MVP, time window, in-memory DB, skip QOL features
  • Get code into a file (not chat)
  • Review the code; find something to change
  • Show active direction—don't blindly accept
Implementation

Interview Signaling

  • Quick recap: Summarize requirements and plan
  • Pivot to code: "Now let me actually get to the code and see what it looks like"
  • Show engagement: Read, evaluate, spot improvements
  • Signal: This demonstrates your coding knowledge and critical thinking
Implementation

Advanced Strategies

  • Split work along component boundaries — spin up separate agents (e.g., API + UI) in parallel
  • Tell AI to make its own decisions — give autonomy on components (risky but shows confidence)
Testing

The Approach

  • Ask AI to write tests for core functionality
  • Focus on happy path and main flows
  • Get tests into a runnable state (actual file)
  • Review test inputs critically: spot gaps, ask AI to improve
  • Add edge cases based on your domain knowledge
  • Run tests and iterate
Testing

Interview Signaling

  • Tell AI to write different types: unit, integration, regression
  • Signal: You understand test strategy, not just "write tests"
  • Show you're thinking about different aspects of validation
Testing

Advanced Strategies

  • Focus on integration tests — where you control and demonstrate the system working as a whole
  • Parallel testing tasks — spin up AI for comprehensive tests in background

Advanced Techniques

  • Voice mode: 150-180 wpm—signals tool mastery and understanding of AI inference
  • Parallelism: Split work across agents or tabs
  • Strategic depth: Spend more time on plan when you know it works
  • Autonomy: Tell AI to make its own decisions on components
