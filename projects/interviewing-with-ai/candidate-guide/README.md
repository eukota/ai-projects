# Candidate Guide

How to perform well in AI-assisted technical interviews.

This guide teaches you the practical strategy for solving interview problems with AI, demonstrating your competency, and managing interview time constraints.

## Universal Principles & Techniques

These apply across all three phases:

### Core Principles

1. **You actively direct the AI** — push back, reject, prioritize. You're designing; AI is the companion, not the decision-maker
2. **Articulate your thinking out loud** — as you work through the problem, speak what you're gaining/learning
3. **Work through the problem WITH the AI** — collaborate, not outsource. This is the core philosophy
4. **Control the narrative** — Guide the interview toward a solvable problem through your questions and choices. Example: ask constraining questions that intentionally narrow scope, so the problem is completable in the time available
5. **Ask AI to explain and compare to familiar concepts** — When you encounter something unclear, ask the AI to explain it. Request comparisons to patterns or concepts you already know to accelerate understanding and build mental models

### Reading AI Responses (tactical techniques)

- Skim the response (don't feel obligated to read everything)
- Look for lists within the response
- Think critically about proposals
- Form your own opinion
- Don't accept output at face value
- Don't need to read the full response — ask for a summary of skipped parts, or tell AI to ignore pieces you don't want

### Advanced Technique: Voice Mode

Using speech-to-text to interface with AI signals sophisticated tool mastery:

- **Speed:** 150-180 wpm (speech) vs. 40-60 wpm (typing prompts—slowed by thinking while typing)
- **Signals:** comfort with AI, efficiency-focused, advanced skill level
- **The insight:** Awareness that speech-to-text introduces errors AND that the AI may misinterpret the imperfect text shows technical sophistication. Yet you still use it confidently because you understand AI infers meaning from noise and can mostly be trusted to work through it.
- **This demonstrates:** deep understanding of AI capabilities, willingness to work with ambiguity, confidence in the system
