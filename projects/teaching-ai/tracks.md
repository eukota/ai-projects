# Teaching Tracks

## University Colloquium

Likely tied to an invited talk at Cal Poly Humboldt.

Goal:

Inspire students, faculty, and community members to see AI as a practical capability amplifier.

Suggested themes:

- What changed recently?
- Why this is different from prior technology waves
- How to use AI well
- Agents and orchestration
- Local models vs frontier models
- Real examples from active builds
- Growth mindset vs fear mindset

Demo ideas:

- Build something live
- Solve a real technical problem
- Ask AI to explain advanced math simply
- Compare weak prompt vs strong prompt
- Multi-agent task decomposition
- Turn an idea into a project spec in minutes

## AI in the Classroom

Core model:

Every student gets access to an AI instructor during class. The teacher remains leader and facilitator, but each learner has individualized support.

Example student requests:

- Explain like I am 5.
- Give me another example.
- Use an analogy.
- Quiz me.
- Show step by step.
- Explain where I got confused.

Instructor dashboard possibilities:

- Where confusion clustered
- Common misconceptions
- Which students asked for help
- Topics needing reteach
- Quiz performance trends
- Engagement patterns

## AI-Native Course Design

Not AI assisting an existing class, but AI becoming part of the course architecture.

Example structure:

1. Human instructor introduces concept.
2. AI tutor supports individuals.
3. Guided lab uses AI help.
4. Students do project work.
5. Students reflect.
6. Adaptive quiz checks understanding.
7. Analytics inform instructor follow-up.

Possible subjects:

- Intro CS
- Programming
- Math
- DevOps
- Data science
- AI fundamentals
- Writing and revision
- Research methods
