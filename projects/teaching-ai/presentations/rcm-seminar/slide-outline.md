# Practical AI for Families, Teachers, and Students

Audience: Redwood Coast Montessori Community

---

# Slide 1: Welcome

AI is already here.

This session is about using it well, safely, and practically.

---

# Slide 2: What AI Is

In simple terms:

AI tools predict useful next outputs from patterns in data.

This can look like:

- Conversation
- Writing help
- Image generation
- Tutoring
- Planning
- Coding help

---

# Slide 3: What AI Is Good At

- Explaining concepts
- Brainstorming ideas
- Summarizing information
- Practicing skills
- Organizing tasks
- Generating drafts

---

# Slide 4: Parents Can Use AI For

- Meal planning
- Weekly schedules
- Travel planning
- Drafting emails
- Chore systems
- Learning support

---

# Slide 5: Students Can Use AI For

- Explain math another way
- Quiz me one question at a time
- Help me study vocabulary
- Brainstorm stories
- Practice science questions
- Check understanding

---

# Slide 6: Teachers Can Use AI For

- Lesson ideas
- Rubrics
- Reading questions
- Differentiated explanations
- Parent communication drafts
- Activity ideas

---

# Slide 7: Prompting Skills

Teach kids to ask:

- Explain like I'm 10
- Give an example
- Show another way
- Quiz me slowly
- Make this easier
- What did I miss?

---

# Slide 8: Important Boundaries

AI can be wrong.

Always:

- Verify important facts
- Use your own thinking
- Protect privacy
- Ask questions
- Use adult guidance when needed

---

# Slide 9: Hands-On Activity

Try one real task:

- Homework help
- Family planning
- Writing help
- Quiz practice
- Creative project

---

# Slide 10: Closing

AI is a tool.

Like calculators, search engines, and computers, the skill is learning how to use it wisely.
