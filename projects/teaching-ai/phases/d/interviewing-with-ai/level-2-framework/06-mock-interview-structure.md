# Mock Interview Structure

How mock interviews work and what to expect.

## Why Mock Interviews Matter

Reading about interviewing is one thing. **Doing it under pressure with real feedback is everything.**

Mock interviews teach you:
- How to manage time under pressure
- What you actually know vs. what you think you know
- How to communicate in real-time
- Where your gaps are

---

## A Typical Mock Interview

**Duration:** 45-50 minutes

### Structure

**1. Intro (3 min)**
- Interviewer explains the problem
- You ask clarifying questions
- You scope the work together

**2. Requirements & Planning (5-10 min)**
- You ask requirements questions
- You discuss approach
- Maybe sketch a design

**3. Implementation (15-20 min)**
- You implement (with AI help)
- You explain as you go
- You handle issues that come up

**4. Testing (5-10 min)**
- You test your solution
- You find and fix bugs
- You validate edge cases

**5. Discussion (5-10 min)**
- Interviewer asks questions
- You explain your solution
- You discuss trade-offs
- You talk about what you'd do differently

---

## What the Interviewer Is Doing

### They're Not Just Listening

The interviewer is:
- Taking mental notes on how you approach problems
- Watching how you use AI
- Noticing how you communicate
- Observing how you handle pressure
- Assessing your level (Entry/Mid/Senior)
- Deciding if they'd hire you

### They're Not Trying to Trick You

The interviewer wants you to succeed. They're testing your capabilities, not gotcha-ing you.

If you're stuck:
- It's okay to ask for hints
- It's okay to say "I'm not sure, let me think"
- It's okay to ask AI for help

---

## Feedback Format

After the mock interview, the interviewer will give you feedback organized by your rubric:

### AI Awareness/Experience
- "You signaled Entry level. Here's what Mid level would look like..."

### Code Proficiency
- "Your code works. Here's what a more elegant solution could be..."

### AI Proficiency
- "You used AI reactively. Here's how senior level would use it proactively..."

### Requirements
- "You asked good questions. At mid level, you'd also think about..."

### Design & Implementation
- "You understood the code. At senior level, you'd drive the design more..."

### Testing
- "You tested the happy path. At mid level, you'd also consider..."

## How to Prepare

### Before the Mock

1. **Review the framework**
   - Read all 5 sections (AI Awareness → Testing)
   - Know what Entry/Mid/Senior looks like

2. **Practice your AI story**
   - Know your 2-minute background
   - Be ready to explain your experience

3. **Get rest**
   - You'll do better when not tired
   - Mental performance matters in interviews

### During the Mock

1. **Think out loud**
   - Explain your reasoning
   - Name things as you go
   - Ask clarifying questions

2. **Use AI strategically**
   - Ask for plans before implementation
   - Review code before accepting it
   - Iterate if there are issues

3. **Manage your time**
   - Don't spend 20 minutes on requirements
   - Don't spend 30 minutes coding
   - Leave time for testing and discussion

4. **Handle pressure**
   - Breathe
   - Go slow
   - Explain what you're doing

### After the Mock

1. **Listen to feedback**
   - Take notes
   - Ask clarifying questions
   - Don't get defensive

2. **Identify gaps**
   - Where did you miss opportunities?
   - What would you do differently next time?
   - What do you need to practice?

3. **Plan next steps**
   - Do another mock if you need it
   - Focus on your gaps
   - Practice the things that felt weak

## What Success Looks Like

### Entry Level Success
- ✓ You asked requirements questions
- ✓ You got code working
- ✓ You tested basic cases
- ✓ You explained what you were doing

### Mid Level Success
- ✓ You thoughtfully scoped the problem
- ✓ You planned before implementing
- ✓ You understood and reviewed code
- ✓ You tested multiple cases
- ✓ You explained your reasoning

### Senior Level Success
- ✓ You drove the design
- ✓ You used AI as a tool you control
- ✓ You made intentional trade-offs
- ✓ You tested strategically
- ✓ You explained everything confidently

## Common Mock Interview Issues

### "I ran out of time"

**Solution:** You need to practice time management. In the mock, track time:
- Requirements: 5 min
- Planning: 5 min
- Implementation: 20 min (move faster)
- Testing: 5 min
- Discussion: 5 min

### "I didn't understand what the interviewer asked"

**Solution:** Ask more clarifying questions. Better to ask "Is this what you meant?" than assume.

### "I felt nervous and made mistakes"

**Solution:** This is normal. Nervous interviews are still learning.
- Practice more mocks (you get better)
- Slow down during the real interview
- Explain what you're doing (reduces anxiety)

### "The interviewer asked questions I couldn't answer"

**Solution:** That's fine. Saying "I'm not sure, let me think" is better than guessing.

## After Your Mock: Next Steps

1. **Do 1-2 more mocks** (especially if you're Entry level)
2. **Focus on your gaps** (whatever the interviewer noted)
3. **Practice communication** (explaining your thinking)
4. **Build confidence** (each mock gets easier)
