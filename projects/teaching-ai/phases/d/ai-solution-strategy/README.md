# Interviewing with AI

Evaluating engineer performance in AI-assisted technical interviews.

## Structure

This section contains a rubric for assessing candidates in interviews where AI is a tool they use during problem-solving.

### Top-Level Rubric Sections

1. **AI Awareness/Experience** — What they know about AI, models, tools, and their track record
2. **Code Proficiency** — Traditional coding skills (same as non-AI interviews)
3. **AI Proficiency** — How well they interface with and drive AI (new to AI interviews)

### Nested: Solution Generation with AI

How they solve problems using AI:

1. **Requirements** — How they gather and clarify requirements
2. **Design and Implementation** — How they plan, design, and code (with AI)
3. **Testing** — How they validate their solution

- **Training recommendation:** Do they need training? (Yes/No)
- **Next steps:** Where to start in the curriculum (Phase/Level)
- **Examples:** Concrete examples of each level

## Key Insights

**Resume-Interview Alignment:** A candidate who claims 2 years of AI experience but only knows ChatGPT chat mode shows misalignment. This is a credibility signal.

**Progression isn't linear:** Engineers may move through Yegge's 8 stages quickly (3 stages in a day) or plateau on one stage for months. Both are normal.

**AI proficiency is learnable:** Unlike raw intelligence or work ethic, AI proficiency is a skill that can be explicitly taught and improved.
