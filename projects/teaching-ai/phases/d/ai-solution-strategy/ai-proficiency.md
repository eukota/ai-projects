# AI Proficiency

How well the candidate interfaces with and drives AI. This is NEW to AI interviews—it measures their actual capability and growth trajectory.

---

## Non-Passing

**Demonstrated knowledge:**
- Don't understand how to prompt AI
- Afraid of using AI
- Spend 20+ minutes writing a single prompt
- Prompts are way too long
- Just accept anything AI says without verification
- No effective interaction with AI

**Interviewer observations:**
- Candidate is hesitant or afraid
- Writing unclear, unfocused prompts
- No iteration or refinement
- Taking AI output blindly
- Wasting time on poor prompting

**Do they need training?** Yes

**Where to start:** Phase C, Level 4 (Consumer)

---

## Entry

**Demonstrated knowledge:**
- Write medium-sized prompts
- Read through AI output
- Don't accept everything at face value
- Do some verification of output
- Can iterate a little

**Interviewer observations:**
- Candidate reads and thinks about AI output
- Asks clarifying questions
- Shows skepticism of AI answers
- Can work with AI but not efficiently

**Do they need training?** Yes

**Where to start:** Phase C, Levels 5-6 (Conversational, Task Agents)

---

## Mid

**Demonstrated knowledge:**
- Interact a lot with AI
- Write short, focused prompts
- Iterate and refine with AI
- Ask AI questions and have it grill them
- Apply skills (MCPs, agents, etc.)
- Broad awareness of modes (planning vs agent vs subagent)
- Know models and their strengths
- Have preferences for which model when

**Interviewer observations:**
- Candidate is comfortable with AI
- Quick iteration cycles
- Good prompt crafting
- Strategic model selection
- Can apply AI primitives

**Do they need training?** Maybe

**Where they are:** Phase C, Levels 6-7 (Task Agents, Sidekick)

---

## Senior

**Demonstrated knowledge:**
- Upper end of Yegge's levels (Levels 8-11)
- Spawn background tasks with separate prompts
- Flip between multiple agents/subagents
- Meta-prompting strategically
- Know what kinds of prompts cause problems
- Strategic about scope and efficiency

**Examples of senior-level meta-prompting:**
- "Optimize for time and leave out edge cases"
- "Use in-memory database only to speed us up"
- "Skip the README file for now"
- "Create a plan but don't implement yet"
- "Let's use three agents: one for design, one for testing, one for docs"

**Interviewer observations:**
- Candidate has predesigned agents or MCPs
- Strategic about complexity vs time
- Fast iteration cycles
- Clear understanding of AI capabilities and limits
- Continuously learning and adapting

**Do they need training?** No

**Where they are:** Phase C, Levels 9-11 (Automation, Orchestration, Autonomy); Phase D (Frontier)

---

## Key Insight

**AI Proficiency measures how far they've progressed through Yegge's 8 stages AND whether they're still growing.**

A candidate could be stuck at Level 5 (Conversational) for 2 years without growth = low AI proficiency.

A candidate who's reached Level 9 (Automation) and still learning = high AI proficiency.

---

## Interview Script Tips

**Entry-level questions:**
- "What's your process for writing a prompt?"
- "How do you verify if AI got something wrong?"

**Mid-level questions:**
- "Do you have a pattern for which model you use?"
- "Tell me about how you iterate with AI"
- "Have you used agents or subagents?"

**Senior-level questions:**
- "Tell me about the agents or MCPs you've built"
- "How do you optimize prompts for speed vs quality?"
- "What's your meta-prompting strategy?"
- "Show me a time you spawned background tasks"
