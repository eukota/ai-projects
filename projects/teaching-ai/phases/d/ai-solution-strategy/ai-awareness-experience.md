# AI Awareness/Experience

What the candidate knows about AI, models, tools, and their actual experience with them.

**This section is verified during the interview.** Resume claims must align with demonstrated knowledge.

---

## Non-Passing

**Demonstrated knowledge:**
- No model awareness (can't distinguish Claude vs GPT vs others)
- Little to no actual AI usage
- Only used AI in chat mode
- Would clearly struggle solving a problem with AI live in an interview

**Interviewer observations:**
- Candidate avoids or is very hesitant about using AI
- Can't name models or explain differences
- No concrete examples of AI work
- Resume claims don't match demonstrated knowledge

**Do they need training?** Yes

**Where to start:** Phase A or Phase B (foundational)

---

## Entry

**Demonstrated knowledge:**
- Basic model awareness (knows Claude, ChatGPT, maybe one other)
- Uses Copilot, ChatGPT, or Cursor regularly
- Basic understanding of models, but not deep
- Writes functional but messy prompts
- Can work with AI but with limitations

**Interviewer observations:**
- Candidate can use AI without major friction
- Knows a couple of tools/models
- Asks reasonable questions
- Can generate code with AI and review it

**Do they need training?** Yes

**Where to start:** Phase C, Levels 4-5 (Consumer, Conversational)

---

## Mid

**Demonstrated knowledge:**
- Good model awareness (knows strengths/weaknesses of different models)
- Aware of how capabilities shifted (Dec 2025, etc.)
- Writes decent prompts that work
- BUT: Forgets critical directives ("use in-memory DB", "skip the review")
- Not fully optimizing for context (interview scenario)

**Interviewer observations:**
- Candidate is comfortable with multiple models
- Has opinions on which model for which task
- Knows about planning, agents, maybe subagents
- Can iterate with AI
- Prompt quality is decent but not optimal

**Do they need training?** Maybe (depends on depth)

**Where to start:** Phase C, Levels 6-7 (Task Agents, Sidekick)

---

## Senior

**Demonstrated knowledge:**
- Expert-level model awareness and model selection strategy
- Understands AI history and capability inflection points
- Writing their own agents or skills
- Aware of the full skills landscape and what different skills can do
- Killer prompt skills
- Meta-prompting effectively

**Interviewer observations:**
- Candidate has predesigned agents or MCPs
- Fires off background tasks with separate prompts
- Uses speech-to-text and other efficiency tools
- Strategic about scope ("for MVP, skip edge cases")
- Can name and use multiple models strategically
- Continuously learning and evolving

**Examples of senior-level meta-prompting:**
- "Optimize for time and leave out edge cases"
- "Use in-memory database only to speed us up"
- "Skip the README file"
- "Create a plan but don't implement yet"

**Do they need training?** No

**Where they are:** Phase C, Levels 8-11 (Swarms through Autonomy) or Phase D (Frontier)

---

## Red Flags: Resume-Interview Misalignment

**Example 1:**
- Resume: "2 years of AI experience"
- Interview reality: Only uses ChatGPT chat mode, no model awareness
- Signal: Low credibility; overselling experience

**Example 2:**
- Resume: "Expert in AI-assisted development"
- Interview reality: Writes 20-minute prompts, can't iterate
- Signal: Mismatch between claimed and actual capability

---

## Interview Script Tips

**Entry-level questions:**
- "What AI tools do you use regularly?"
- "Can you explain the difference between Claude and GPT?"
- "Tell me about a time you got a wrong answer from AI. How did you handle it?"

**Mid-level questions:**
- "What's your process for prompting AI?"
- "Are you aware of how AI capabilities changed recently?"
- "Do you use different models for different tasks? Why?"

**Senior-level questions:**
- "Tell me about an agent or system you've built with AI"
- "How do you optimize your prompts for speed vs. quality?"
- "Have you built any AI primitives (MCPs, skills, etc.)?"
