## Known Approaches

### Evaluating AI Usage in Code Interviews

The concept of assessing how well candidates work WITH AI (not just replacing traditional coding skills) is gaining traction across:

- **Pair programming interviews** — Evaluating collaboration dynamics
- **Open-book interviews** — Where AI tools are allowed/encouraged
- **Live coding with AI** — Assessing AI-assisted problem solving
- **AI proficiency tracks** — Some companies now have separate "AI engineer" hiring tracks

### Common Themes Across Companies

Based on industry observation, companies evaluating AI in interviews tend to focus on:

1. **Tool proficiency** — Can they use AI effectively?
2. **Judgment** — Do they make smart decisions about when/how to use AI?
3. **Verification** — Can they validate AI output?
4. **Communication** — Can they work with AI AND explain to humans?
5. **Learning velocity** — Are they improving their AI practices?

## Observations

**This is emerging territory.** As of early 2026:

1. **No universal standard yet** — Companies are developing their own approaches
2. **Rapid iteration** — Methods are changing quickly as AI capability evolves
3. **Common patterns** — Despite differences, similar themes emerge (judgment, tool proficiency, verification)
4. **Public interest** — Engineering communities are actively discussing this
