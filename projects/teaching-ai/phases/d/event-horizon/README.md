# Event Horizon: Self-Learning and Agentic Evolution

Exploring the frontier of agents that self-improve, adapt, and evolve beyond initial programming.

## What This Is About

Beyond orchestrating agents is a question that haunts AI engineering: what happens when agents begin to shape their own improvement? This section explores the philosophical and technical territory where agents become increasingly autonomous in their learning, adaptation, and strategic choices.

**Note:** This is territory on the horizon—some parts speculative, some parts emerging in research and practice. We're documenting both what's possible and what questions we're asking about it.

## Core Questions

**Self-Learning & Adaptation**
- How do agents learn from interaction and feedback?
- What mechanisms enable agents to update their own strategies?
- Can agents recognize when to shift approaches without external direction?

**Model Selection & Capability Matching**
- What kinds of models enable agentic self-improvement?
- How do agents choose between different models or tools as they evolve?
- What's the relationship between model scale, reasoning, and agentic autonomy?

**Emergent Behavior**
- What behaviors emerge when agents have agency over their own learning?
- How do we recognize and measure emergence in agentic systems?
- What are the boundaries of intentional design vs. emergent properties?

**Philosophical Frontiers**
- What does autonomy mean for an AI agent?
- At what point does an agent system become qualitatively different from its programming?
- How do we think about agency, intention, and responsibility?

## The Horizon

Some of these questions don't have answers yet. The goal here is to map the territory, understand what's being explored at the research frontier, and prepare for the kinds of systems we might build in the next era of AI.
