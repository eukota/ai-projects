# Self-Assessment

How to use the rubric to understand where you stand.

## The Rubric Sections

Your interviewer evaluates you on 6 areas:

1. **AI Awareness/Experience** — What you know and have done with AI
2. **Code Proficiency** — Your coding skills
3. **AI Proficiency** — How well you use AI as a tool
4. **Requirements Gathering** — How you scope and clarify work
5. **Design & Implementation** — How you plan and code
6. **Testing** — How you validate your solution

Each area has 4 levels: Non-Passing, Entry, Mid, Senior.

---

## Self-Assessment Exercise

After a mock interview (or before preparing), assess yourself honestly.

### For Each Area, Ask:

**1. AI Awareness/Experience**
- Can I talk about my AI experience without pauses?
- Can I name and describe different models?
- Do I have real work examples, not just chat?
- Am I at Entry, Mid, or Senior level?

**2. Code Proficiency**
- Can I explain code I wrote?
- Can I trace through logic?
- Do I understand my implementation?
- Can I identify potential issues?

**3. AI Proficiency**
- Do I use AI reactively or strategically?
- Can I plan before implementing?
- Do I review code or just accept it?
- Do I iterate and improve?

**4. Requirements Gathering**
- Do I ask clarifying questions?
- Do I think about scope and trade-offs?
- Do I engage with the problem or just code?
- Do I scope the work before starting?

**5. Design & Implementation**
- Do I understand my design?
- Can I explain my choices?
- Do I use AI effectively?
- Can I improve my solution?

**6. Testing**
- Do I test beyond happy path?
- Do I think about edge cases?
- Do I validate my solution?
- Can I use tests to find bugs?

## What Does "Ready for Interview" Mean?

**Minimum:** Entry level across all areas

**Good:** Entry → Mid on most areas

**Strong:** Mid on most areas, Senior on some

**Excellent:** Mid or Senior across all areas

---

## Identifying Your Gaps

### If You're Mostly Entry Level

**Focus areas:**
1. Build real AI work examples (not just chat)
2. Practice explaining your thinking
3. Ask more questions in mock interviews
4. Test more thoroughly

**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks of practice

### If You're Mixed Entry/Mid

**Focus areas:**
1. Identify which areas are weakest
2. Target those in your next mock
3. Practice communication (explain everything)
4. Do 2-3 mocks to build confidence

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks of practice

### If You're Mostly Mid Level

**Focus areas:**
1. Push from Mid → Senior in 1-2 areas
2. Make intentional design decisions (own it)
3. Use AI proactively, not reactively
4. Explain trade-offs confidently

**Timeline:** 1 week of focused practice

### If You're Mid or Senior Across All Areas

**You're ready.** Do 1 mock to shake out nerves, then interview.

---

## Practice Plan

Based on your assessment, build a practice plan:

### Week 1
- [ ] Do a mock interview
- [ ] Get feedback on all 6 areas
- [ ] Identify 2-3 weakest areas

### Week 2
- [ ] Focus practice on weak areas
- [ ] Do another mock interview
- [ ] Compare to Week 1 — did you improve?

### Week 3
- [ ] Polish strong areas
- [ ] Do final mock (for confidence)
- [ ] Review rubric one more time
- [ ] Ready to interview

---

## Red Flags: Are You Honest?

**If you scored yourself Mid or Senior on everything:**

You might not be honest with yourself. Check by:
- Can you explain AI use strategically? (not just chat)
- Do you understand every line of code you wrote?
- Can you articulate design trade-offs?
- Do you test edge cases?

If you can't do these, you're probably Entry or early Mid.

**Honest assessment is better than inflated scores.**

---

## Ready to Interview?

You're ready if:

**✓ Entry level:**
- You can talk about your AI experience
- You can ask clarifying questions
- You can get code working
- You can test basic cases

**✓ Mid level:**
- You can explain your reasoning
- You understand your code
- You can think through trade-offs
- You test thoughtfully

**✓ Senior level:**
- You drive the design
- You explain everything confidently
- You use AI as a tool you control
- You think at systems level

---

## One More Thing: Humility

The best interviews happen when you:
- Know your level (honest self-assessment)
- Know what you don't know (ask questions)
- Are willing to learn (responsive to feedback)
- Can admit mistakes (fix them quickly)

A Mid-level engineer who knows they're Mid-level, asks good questions, and admits when stuck looks better than an Entry-level engineer pretending to be Senior.

## Final Checklist Before Your Interview

- [ ] I've done at least 1 mock interview
- [ ] I know my AI story (can tell it in 2 min)
- [ ] I can explain my design decisions
- [ ] I test thoroughly
- [ ] I communicate clearly
- [ ] I'm honest about my level
- [ ] I know what I need to improve
- [ ] I'm ready to learn from feedback

If you can check all of these, you're ready to interview.
